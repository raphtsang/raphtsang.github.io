Overview

One of the 20th century's greatest, most influential innovations, Sony's Walkman revolutionized the way we listen to music.
While technology has greatly advanced since the conception of the original Walkman in 1979, having such an iconic product to your company's name is something that should be celebrated. This Sony brand refresh aims to bring the feeling of nostalgia to those both young and old by reminding people of the famous Walkman through the use of colour.

Concept

Taking inspiration from Sony's Sports Walkman range, in particular the WM-F45, the iconic yellow and blue colour combination was extracted and paired with the secondary orange colour found on the sticker. Put together with a gradient of lines increasing in weight, the Sony logo has been given a retro yet refreshed look. The bright primary colours contrast with the vibrant orange, giving the logo an extra punch, evoking feel-good emotions and nostalgia that aims to encourage the success of the brand.

Sony's most recent product of their Walkman product line, the NW-WM1A, is only offered in black. Refreshing the colours of the product, the new palette brings the new Walkman back to its roots, and brings wireless capabilities to the classic player while giving users a sense of nostalgia.

Poster

The layout and style of the final poster draws inspiration from one of Sony's own posters from the 1980s. This poster is an example of a common advertising style from that time, which is also around when the Walkman was released. The catchy and bold style of the poster heading remains relevant to this day, with audience attention being increasingly difficult to obtain and maintain, this style translates well in order to capture the eyes of potential customers even now.
