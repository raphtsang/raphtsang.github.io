Skills

Tools

HTML, CSS, Javascript, Adobe InDesign, Adobe XD

Overview

Furniture Galore is a Melbourne-based furniture store with four retail stores around the city. Given their focus on in-person sales, their website has been neglected, and although the website appears to have a quite contemporary and up-to-date design, there are many inconsistencies in the design that ruin the initially pleasing aesthetic of the site.

In fact, perhaps due to combining factors of their badly designed online image and the recent pandemic, the site informed visitors of a closing down sale at the end of the short 12-week span of this project, and is now officially closed. An archived version of the website can be seen here.

Goals

The goal of this project is to redesign Furniture Galore's website to provide users with a much smoother and more enjoyable experience. The main problems are:

1. Many inconsistencies in design ruins the initially pleasing aesthetic of the website.
2. Ambiguities and dark patterns can cause a lot of confusion and easily creates traps for users.
3. Alignment and spacing quite bad, with sections of menu being hard to reach, as well as causing illegibility in some areas and misinformation.
4. Unnecessary information is highlighted, and important information is less visible.
5. Misleading menu and submenu categories that contain no products.
